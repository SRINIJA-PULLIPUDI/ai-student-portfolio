# 2. Permitted Uses

Students may use AI for the following purposes:

1. AI may be used to improve résumé wording, grammar, and formatting while preserving factual accuracy.
2. AI may be used to conduct mock interviews and generate interview practice questions.
3. AI may be used to explain technical concepts and placement-preparation topics for learning purposes.
4. AI may be used to create study schedules, preparation plans, and revision checklists.
5. AI may be used to summarize company information when the student verifies important facts using official company sources.
6. AI may be used to review student-written code and suggest improvements, provided the student can subsequently defend the final solution.

---

# 3. Prohibited Uses

### 1. Fabrication of Experience or Projects

AI may NOT be used to create fake projects, internships, certifications, research work, or employment history.

**Detection Method:** Mentor-led oral defence and project walkthrough requiring explanation of technical decisions and implementation details.

### 2. Manipulation of Academic Records

AI may NOT be used to generate, modify, or misrepresent CGPA, grades, transcripts, rankings, or academic achievements.

**Detection Method:** Verification against official university records and academic portal data.

### 3. Identity Fabrication During Recruitment

AI may NOT be used to clone, modify, synthesize, or imitate a student's voice, image, or identity during placement interviews or assessments.

**Detection Method:** Recruiter verification procedures, live interaction requirements, and identity confirmation checks.

### 4. Undeclared AI Authorship in Personal Assessment Essays

AI may NOT be used to fully generate Statements of Purpose, motivation letters, or application essays where the student's personal voice is the primary assessment criterion.

**Detection Method:** Mentor review using historical writing samples and student explanation of submitted content.

---

# 4. Enforcement

The Placement Cell may conduct random audits, portfolio reviews, and oral defences for placement-related submissions. Violations of prohibited uses may result in a written warning, mandatory re-evaluation, temporary suspension of placement support, or removal from the placement process depending on severity and repetition. The Placement Cell acknowledges that not all AI use is detectable and therefore relies partly on student integrity, professional ethics, and the long-term consequences of misrepresentation.

---

# 5. Policy Principles

1. AI should be used to enhance learning, preparation, and communication.
2. AI should not be used to misrepresent identity, qualifications, or achievements.
3. Students remain responsible for all content submitted to recruiters, regardless of whether AI assistance was used.
